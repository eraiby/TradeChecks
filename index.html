Regime
Market structure classification. Trending = strong directional move with ADX above 25. Ranging = price oscillating without direction. Conflicted = mixed signals across timeframes. Only trade strong directional setups in Trending regimes — from Chan's regime-conditional framework.
Best TF
OI Δ
Open Interest change rate — how much the total number of open futures contracts changed on the last candle. A spike of +3% or more without a matching price move means a large position was opened (direction unknown yet). Red = spike without confirmation. From Binance futures data.
Funding
Funding rate paid every 8 hours between longs and shorts. Above +0.05% = longs severely crowded, squeeze risk. Below −0.05% = shorts severely crowded. Amber = approaching extreme. Red = danger zone — critical override applies regardless of other signals.
RSI Div
RSI Divergence — when price makes a new high/low but RSI does not confirm it. Bullish divergence (↑) = price lower low, RSI higher low — potential reversal up. Bearish divergence (↓) = price higher high, RSI lower high — potential reversal down. Confirmed on closed candles only.
ATR Risk
Average True Range as a percentage of your liquidation buffer. Shows whether natural price volatility will hit your liquidation before your trade plays out. Green = safe. Amber = ATR consuming 50–80% of buffer. Red = ATR exceeds 80% — at this leverage the market's normal breathing room liquidates you.
Score
Opportunity score explained — scored 0–10 from four equally weighted components: Signal conviction (how many timeframes agree and by how much) + Microstructure alignment (does crowd positioning confirm the direction) + Volatility suitability (moderate volatility scores highest — too calm = no movement, too extreme = too risky) + Session quality (is this a good session to trade right now). Assets are sorted by score. Top pick is highlighted in green. — Critical columns (OI Δ, Funding, RSI Div, ATR Risk) show real parameter values and turn red when they breach danger thresholds — a red critical cell means that parameter compromises the score regardless of its aggregate value.

Q6 — What is the evidence behind the direction signal?
?
Left panel shows technical indicators — price action based signals from chart data. Right panel shows microstructure — crowd positioning signals. When both panels agree on direction, confidence is high. When they conflict, investigate before entering. The squeeze warning on the right fires when retail is heavily short but whales are not — historically a high-probability upside signal.
Technical signals
EMA 20/50
—
EMA 20 / 50 Crossover
Exponential Moving Averages smooth out price noise. When the fast EMA (20) crosses above the slow EMA (50), price is trending up. When it crosses below, trend is down. This is the primary trend-direction signal.
Weight: High — 1 of 6 core signals
RSI (14)
—
RSI — Relative Strength Index
Measures how fast price is moving on a 0–100 scale. On short timeframes (1h/4h): above 65 = overbought/exhausted (lean short), below 35 = oversold (lean long). On daily/weekly: above 65 = strong trend momentum (lean long).
Weight: High — timeframe-aware scoring
Bollinger Band
—
Bollinger Bands
A volatility envelope around price (20-period mean ± 2 standard deviations). Price touching the lower band = stretched to downside, potential bounce (long signal). Upper band = stretched upside, potential reversal (short signal). Middle = neutral, no edge.
Weight: Medium — confirms entry timing
⚡ BB squeeze active — low volatility compression, breakout imminent
Volume
—
Volume vs 20-Period Average
Compares current candle volume to the 20-candle average. A price move on high volume (1.5x+) is genuine and likely to continue. A move on low volume is weak and likely to reverse. Volume confirms conviction — without it, signals are unreliable.
Weight: Medium — confirmation only, not directional
Funding rate
—
Funding Rate
Unique to crypto futures. Paid every 8 hours between longs and shorts. Positive rate = longs pay shorts (market is crowded long — potential short squeeze). Negative rate = shorts pay longs (crowded short — potential long squeeze). Extremes above ±0.05% are meaningful.
Weight: High — catches reversals chart signals miss
Liq. sweep
—
Liquidity Sweep
Tracks whether a known liquidity cluster (stop-loss or liquidation pocket) below or above current price has already been swept. If yes, market makers have hunted that liquidity — safer to enter. If unswept, price may dip to hunt it before the real move. Toggle manually using the switch above.
Weight: Medium — manual input required

Crowd positioning
⚠ Squeeze warning
—
Retail L/S
—
—
Retail Long/Short Ratio
% of retail accounts holding longs vs shorts. Retail is typically wrong at extremes. Ratio below 0.8 (many shorting) = squeeze setup — lean long. Ratio above 1.3 (many longing) = crowded — lean short or wait.
Weight: Contrarian — fade the crowd
Whale accounts
—
—
Top Account (Whale) Ratio
Positioning of the top 20% of accounts by size — the smart money. Whales are better informed. When whales long and retail short simultaneously = classic squeeze setup. Always follow whale direction over retail. This divergence is the most reliable squeeze signal.
Weight: Very High — follow the smart money
Taker flow
—
—
Taker Buy/Sell Flow
Ratio of aggressive buy orders (market buys) vs aggressive sell orders over the last 10 candles. Takers are impatient — they hit the ask to buy immediately. Ratio above 1.05 = buyers more aggressive = bullish pressure. Below 0.95 = sellers dominate. Neutral around 1.0.
Weight: Medium — short-term pressure gauge
OI trend
—
—
Open Interest Trend
Total value of open futures contracts. Rising OI = new money entering the market (confirms the move). Falling OI = positions closing (weakening trend, potential reversal). A price rise with falling OI is a warning — the move has no new buyers behind it. OI spike + price spike = strong conviction.
Weight: High — confirms whether move is real
Retail longs %
—
Retail Long/Short Ratio
Percentage of retail accounts holding long positions across all exchanges. Above 55% = longs crowded (fade the retail crowd = lean short). Below 45% = retail heavily short = squeeze setup (lean long). Most reliable when it diverges from whale positioning.
Weight: High — contrarian signal
Whale longs %
—
Whale / Top-Account Ratio
Long/short positioning of the top 20% of accounts by size (whales). Whales are better informed than retail. When whales are long and retail is short — that's a squeeze setup. When they align with retail, signal is weaker. Follow the whales over the crowd.
Weight: Very High — smart money signal
CVD momentum
—
Cumulative Volume Delta (CVD)
Tracks net buying vs selling pressure by comparing taker buy volume to taker sell volume over 20 candles. Positive CVD = buyers are more aggressive than sellers. Negative = sellers dominate. Divergence between CVD and price direction is an early reversal warning.
Weight: Medium — flow confirmation

Q7 — How volatile is this asset across timeframes?
?
Each row shows the ATR (Average True Range) as a percentage of current price for that timeframe, compared to its own 30-period average. Green = calm (below normal), blue = normal, amber = elevated, red = high. If short timeframes are hot but daily is calm, it is noise. If daily and weekly are both high, a major structural move is happening — adjust position size down significantly.
1h
—
—
1h ATR — Hourly Volatility
Average price range per 1-hour candle as a % of current price. This is your minimum stop-loss distance for a 1h trade. If ATR is 0.8%, a stop tighter than 0.8% will get hit by normal noise. Ratio compares today's ATR to the 30-candle average — 1.5x means it's 50% more volatile than usual.
Weight: Sets minimum stop distance for 1h entries
4h
—
—
4h ATR — 4-Hour Volatility
Average price range per 4-hour candle. A typical 4h swing trade needs a stop at least this wide to avoid being stopped out on routine moves. If 4h ATR is elevated but 1D ATR is calm, the intraday noise is high — consider waiting for a calmer entry window.
Weight: Sets minimum stop for 4h swing entries
1D
—
—
Daily ATR — Day-Level Volatility
Average daily price range as % of price. This is how much the asset typically moves in a full day. High daily ATR means a leveraged position can get liquidated by a single normal trading day. Compare this to your liquidation distance — if they're close, your leverage is too high.
Weight: Key for leverage safety — compare to liq. distance
1W
—
—
Weekly ATR — Swing Volatility
Average weekly price range. This is the expected move range for a multi-day position. Weekly ATR above 15% means swing trades carry high risk — the asset can reverse its entire weekly range before your target is hit. Used as the 2× ATR default target in the simulator.
Weight: Sets realistic target range for multi-day holds
1M
—
—
Monthly ATR — Structural Volatility
Average monthly price range. Indicates the macro volatility regime. High monthly ATR means the asset is in a structural trend phase — large directional moves, wide swings. Use this to calibrate maximum leverage: if monthly ATR is 40%, never use leverage above 2–3x on any timeframe.
Weight: Sets maximum safe leverage for the current regime
YTD
—
—
YTD ATR — Year-to-Date Volatility
ATR since January 1st of the current year. Gives context for whether current volatility is high or low relative to this year's baseline. If YTD ratio is well above 1.0, the current period is more volatile than the year average — tighten leverage. If below 1.0, conditions are calmer than usual.
Weight: Annual baseline context — is today unusual?

Q9 — Does my risk setup pass the safety check?
?
Three independent checks must all pass before entering. Market condition checks whether the broader environment is hostile. Signal strength checks whether enough technical factors agree. Risk gate checks whether your leverage and position size are within safe limits. All three must show green or amber before you trade.
Market condition —
—
Fear & Greed
—
Fear & Greed Index
Crypto market sentiment on a 0–100 scale. Below 25 = Extreme Fear (historically good for buying — everyone is panicking). Above 75 = Extreme Greed (crowded longs, potential reversal coming). Between 40–60 = neutral market, trade technicals. Updated daily by alternative.me.
Weight: Bonus factor — environmental context
Session quality
—
Trading Session Quality
Different market sessions have different liquidity. London/NY overlap (8am–12pm EST) = highest volume, cleanest signals, tightest spreads. Tokyo = medium, good for DOGE/XRP. Sydney = lowest liquidity, unreliable signals, wide spreads. Score: 3 = prime, 2 = good, 1 = medium, 0 = low.
Weight: High — bad session = false signals
BTC macro
—
BTC Macro Trend (EMA 20/50 on 1h)
Bitcoin drives 60–80% of alt-coin price movement. When BTC's 1h EMA 20 is above EMA 50, the macro environment is bullish — longs on alts are higher probability. When BTC is bearish, avoid longs on alts regardless of their own chart signals. This is the single most important context indicator on the dashboard.
Weight: Very High — overrides alt-coin signals

Tech signals
—
Technical Signal Score
Aggregate of all 6 technical indicators (EMA, RSI, Bollinger Band, Volume, Funding, Liq Sweep). Each votes +1 long, -1 short, or 0 neutral. Score is shown as how many of 6 are aligned. 4+ in agreement = high conviction. 2–3 = wait for confirmation. Below 2 = no edge, stay out.
Weight: Core decision signal
Microstructure
—
Microstructure Score
Aggregate of crowd positioning signals (Retail L/S, Whale ratio, Taker flow, OI trend, CVD). When microstructure confirms the technical direction, conviction is high. When they conflict — e.g. technicals say long but retail is heavily long too — be cautious. Contrarian signal: fade the retail crowd.
Weight: High — independent confirmation layer
Risk gate —
—
Leverage
—
Leverage Safety Check
Your current leverage setting vs the safe limit of 15x. Below 10x = safe (green). 10–15x = caution (amber). Above 15x = BLOCKED — the risk gate will prevent entry. At 20x your liquidation is only 5% away. At 100x it's 1% — DOGE moves 1% every few minutes.
Weight: Hard gate — blocks trade if exceeded
Liq. distance
—
Liquidation Distance
How far price needs to move against you before you are forcibly liquidated. Calculated as 100% ÷ leverage. At 5x = 20% buffer (safe). At 10x = 10% (moderate). At 20x = 5% (dangerous — BTC regularly moves 5% in an hour). This is the most important number to check before entering any leveraged trade.
Weight: Hard gate — reality check
Volatility ATR
—
ATR — Average True Range
Measures how much price moves on average per candle in dollar terms. High ATR relative to its own average means volatility is elevated — your stop needs to be wider or you'll get stopped out by noise. ATR ratio above 2x average = reduce position size. Above 3x = consider sitting out entirely.
Weight: Position sizing — adjusts stop distance
